The History of Kare-kare, According to the Kapampangans

Kare-kare's storied history as a Filipino food goes back hundreds of years, with at least three ethnic groups claiming they invented it. A very close inspection of its name led us to some fascinating discoveries: that kare-kare was a Kapampangan term that meant poor imitation of their version of kari (curry), that its name was the basis of the word karinderya, and that kari meant sauce in other Asian languages.

Kare-kare has been with the Filipinos for hundreds of years. The kare-kare of today is ox-tail and tripe immersed in an achiote-tinted, peanut-flavored gravy, served with blanched vegetables on a separate platter, and shrimp paste served on the side. Its hearty reputation has led it to become the star of town fiestas, family potlucks, and Mama Sita commercial ads. Kare-kare evokes culinary elegance, tradition, and home.
